Key Findings
  • From 2010 to 2020, the majority of fires occur during the fall season, from August to November.
  • The primary factor influencing understory fire is fire size.
  • The deforestation fire has the highest biomass value, the highest fire radiative power, and the longest fire persistence.
  • The size of the fire has a minor impact on high fire confidence.
  • Fire persistence has a significant impact on all types of fire confidence.
  • From 2018 to 2019, the deforested area reached a decade high of 259,349 square kilometers. In other words, the rate of deforestation has increased by 34% in a year.
  • State Rondonia has the highest accumulated deforested area until the year 2020, accounting for 23 percent of its total.
  • In the year 2020, the Amazon rainforest has an estimated 230,679 fires occurred. And most of the fires happened in states Para and Maranhao.
  • The fire has spread to 93,096 square kilometers in the state of Mato Grosso. In 2020, this state had the most burning area compared to the other states.
  • The states of Para and Mato Grosso have the most fires that last longer than three days.
